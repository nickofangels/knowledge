# Cross-Domain Connections

Non-obvious links between knowledge domains, surfaced 2026-04-06.

---

## 1. ADHD bridges health science and design practice

**personal-health** covers ADHD & sleep at the neurobiological level. **adhd-design** translates that into UX constraints: auto-save, drift tolerance, momentum-focused interfaces. These aren't two separate topics — they're the same problem (executive function variability) viewed from the biological and applied sides. Sleep quality findings in personal-health directly inform what "drift tolerance" needs to accommodate in adhd-design.

## 2. Context management is the same problem for brains and agents

**claude-code** deals with session management, compaction, and context windows. **adhd-design** deals with drift tolerance, auto-save, and progressive disclosure. Both are solving the same fundamental problem: *how to maintain coherent state when working memory is limited and lossy*. Compaction is the AI version of progressive disclosure. Session management is the machine equivalent of momentum-focused UX. Design patterns from one domain could directly inform the other.

## 3. Polarized training mirrors model routing

**physical-performance** documents polarized training — the principle that optimal adaptation comes from spending time at either very low or very high intensity, skipping the middle. **claude-code** and **agent-workflows** both describe model routing — sending tasks to either cheap/fast models or expensive/capable ones. This is the same optimization insight applied to biology vs. compute: *the middle is where you waste resources for the least return*.

## 4. Tiered resource allocation recurs across domains

- **personal-finance**: 3-tier cost-cutting framework
- **agent-workflows**: 4-layer agent workflow framework
- **physical-performance**: polarized training zones
- **claude-code**: model routing tiers

Four different domains independently arrived at tiered/layered frameworks for deciding where to allocate scarce resources (money, compute, training stress, model capability). The underlying principle — *categorize inputs by impact tier, then apply different strategies per tier* — is domain-agnostic.

## 5. Inflammatory pathways connect skin, joints, and exercise recovery

**personal-health** covers inflammatory pathways and skincare science. **physical-performance** covers connective tissue adaptation and training recovery. Systemic inflammation is the shared mechanism: the same inflammatory cascades that affect skin conditions also govern tendon/ligament repair and exercise recovery timelines. An anti-inflammatory protocol researched in one domain has direct implications for the other.

## 6. Peptides sit at the health-performance intersection

**personal-health** includes peptides as a topic. **physical-performance** covers connective tissue and mitochondrial adaptation. Peptides like BPC-157 and thymosin beta-4 act on tissue repair pathways that are central to exercise recovery. This isn't two domains coincidentally covering related chemistry — it's one biological system (tissue repair and adaptation) split across two knowledge silos.

## 7. Mitochondria are the hidden link between sleep, fat oxidation, and longevity

**personal-health** covers ADHD & sleep. **physical-performance** covers mitochondrial adaptation, fat oxidation, and longevity. Sleep quality directly regulates mitochondrial function, which determines fat oxidation capacity, which is a core marker for metabolic health and longevity. The causal chain runs: sleep → mitochondria → fat oxidation → longevity. This thread connects articles across both domains that may not reference each other.

## 8. Web development and personal finance share an infrastructure layer

**web-development** includes headless Shopify architecture. **personal-finance** includes VPS deployment patterns and privacy-first app architecture. Both deal with hosting, deployment, and web infrastructure — but one is oriented toward client/commercial work and the other toward personal cost optimization. The VPS deployment knowledge likely originated from optimizing hosting costs for web-development projects, making personal-finance partly a spin-off.

## 9. The knowledge base itself is an agent workflow

The entire repository structure — raw sources → compilation → wiki articles → audits — mirrors the **agent-workflows** 4-layer framework. The compile procedure is an agent pipeline. The knowledge-check audit is a validation layer. The CLAUDE.md files are system prompts. This repo isn't just *about* agent workflows; it *is* one. Meta-improvements to agent-workflows could be tested by improving the knowledge compilation process itself.

## 10. Peak moments connect personal development to singing

**personal-development** includes peak moments analysis as part of the Catalyst 88 / Find Your Why framework. **singing** covers performance. Peak moments in personal development are often *performance* moments — the methodology for identifying them likely surfaces singing/performance experiences as data points. The two domains feed each other: understanding *why* certain performances felt meaningful (personal-development) informs *what* to prioritize in practice and repertoire (singing).

## 11. Proof packs are the agent equivalent of evidence.md

**agent-workflows** describes proof packs as a deliverable/verification pattern. Every domain in the knowledge base has an `evidence.md` file for dated observations. These serve the same function: providing traceable evidence chains that support claims. The proof pack concept from agent-workflows is already implemented as a pattern across the entire knowledge base — it just isn't named that way.

## 12. Voice/tone enforcement is a constraint pattern that recurs

**claude-code** covers voice/tone enforcement for AI outputs. **adhd-design** covers progressive disclosure and momentum-focused UX — which are also about controlling *how* information is presented. Both are constraint systems that shape output without changing content. The principles behind voice/tone enforcement (rule hierarchies, override precedence, contextual adaptation) could be applied to UX copy patterns in adhd-design.
